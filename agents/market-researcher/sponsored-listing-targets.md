## Selection Criteria

- Companies with **commercial MCP tools** (not hobbyist/individual repos)
- Companies that already **invest in developer marketing** (DevRel teams, ecosystem sponsorships)
- Tools in **competitive categories** where placement visibility drives decisions
- Mix of **well-funded startups** and **established players**
- Ranked by **likelihood to convert** (budget signal + GTM fit + decision speed + competitive pressure)

## Pitch Templates

### "Your competitor ranks higher" (urgency)
For: Qdrant, Exa, Neon, MongoDB
> "We index every MCP tool on GitHub and rank them by real usage signals. Right now [Competitor] ranks [N] spots ahead of your tool. A sponsored listing ensures your tool appears first in its category for AI developers making build-vs-buy decisions."

### "Lock in your position" (defense)
For: Manufact, Stacklok, Perplexity, SerpApi, Prefect
> "You're already in the top [N] on AgentRank. The ecosystem is growing fast — 100+ new tools indexed every week. A sponsored listing locks your category position as the index scales."

### "Fresh capital, developer reach" (timing)
For: Qdrant (Series B March 2026), Tavily (Series A Aug 2025), Exa (Series B Sep 2025)
> "You just closed [round]. The fastest way to convert that into developer mindshare is being visibly top-ranked on the index that AI builders consult. $[X]/mo on AgentRank is DevRel budget, not a board decision."

### "Star/rank gap" (insight)
For: 21st.dev (4,469 stars, rank #591), Notion (4,059 stars, rank #209)
> "You have more GitHub stars than 99% of our index, yet you're ranked #[X]. That gap exists because our ranking measures maintainer responsiveness and dependency depth, not just star count. A sponsored listing closes the perception gap."

### "Category ownership" (first-mover)
For: Bright Data, Apollo GraphQL, Sentry, Stacklok
> "Your tool is the only serious [category] MCP in our entire index of 25,000+ tools. A sponsored listing makes that official — every developer searching for [category] tooling sees you first."
