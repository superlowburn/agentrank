_Version 1.0 — March 2026_

## 1. Target Profile

**Who pays for sponsored listings?**

The ideal prospect is a company (not an individual dev) that has built an MCP tool as part of their commercial product strategy. They need developer mindshare, and developer discovery is a direct input to their revenue or growth.

**Primary targets:**

- **VC-backed dev tool companies** — Early-stage to Series B companies building MCP-native products. They have marketing budget, care about developer adoption metrics, and understand distribution. CAC from sponsored placements is a line item they can justify.
- **Enterprise software vendors adding AI/MCP integrations** — Companies like Perplexity, Stacklok, or NeMo (NVIDIA) that build MCP servers as a moat or API gateway. They compete for developer attention in crowded categories.
- **Commercial open-source projects with paid tiers** — Tools that are free on GitHub but sell support, cloud hosting, or a Pro tier (e.g., fastmcp/PrefectHQ model). Sponsoring the category page drives qualified signups.
- **Platform companies expanding into MCP** — Microsoft Azure, Google Workspace, Vercel, Cloudflare. They sponsor to signal ecosystem leadership, not just drive installs.

**Disqualifiers:**

- Solo maintainers with no revenue model (wrong product for them)
- Archived or unmaintained repos
- Tools without English documentation (hard to close, bad fit)
- Projects where the org has no budget authority (academic, pure open source nonprofits)

## 2. Value Proposition

**What they get from a Sponsored Listing on AgentRank:**

| Tier | Price | Placement |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Category Sponsor | $199/mo | Top of one category page |
| Featured Sponsor | $299/mo | Category + homepage sidebar |

**The pitch — what makes this worth $199-499/mo:**

1. **Intent-qualified traffic** — Developers landing on AgentRank are actively evaluating MCP tools. This is not programmatic ad traffic. Every pageview is a potential user comparing your tool to alternatives.

2. **Category ownership** — You don't just get placement — you own the top position in your category. If a developer searches "Kubernetes MCP", your tool is what they see first, with a Sponsored label.

3. **Real analytics** — Views, click-throughs, comparison events. You can see exactly how many developers compared your tool to competitors in any given month.

4. **Slack access for updates** — Sponsored customers get a shared Slack channel. When your score changes or a new competitor enters your category, we tell you.

5. **Newsletter reach** (Premium only) — AgentRank newsletter goes to MCP builders and decision-makers. A feature here is a warm intro to an audience that doesn't see banner ads.

**Anchor comparison:** A single GitHub Sponsors post from a well-known developer can cost $500/mo with no performance data. A sponsored post on a dev newsletter like TLDR Dev or Console runs $2,000-5,000 per send, untargeted. AgentRank gives you persistent, intent-qualified placement at $199/mo with real attribution.

## 4. Objection Handling

**"We don't pay for directory listings."**

> Fair. Most directories are traffic farms you're paying to be listed in. AgentRank is different — you're already in the index for free. The sponsored placement is about owning your category in front of developers who are actively comparing tools. The question isn't whether it's worth being listed, it's whether top-of-category placement in front of intent-qualified traffic is worth $199/mo to you.

---

**"Our tool is open source. We don't have a marketing budget."**

> Got it. This is really a fit for commercial tools or projects with a paid offering. If that changes — or if you have a Pro tier, hosted version, or support contract — come back to us. The placement makes most sense when you have something to convert visitors into.

---

**"How do we know this isn't just paying for fake traffic?"**

> Good question. All our analytics are from real pageviews — we separate bot traffic from human traffic (it's in our stack). Before you commit, I can share the actual category page visit numbers so you can decide if the volume justifies the spend. We don't lock you in on annual — monthly billing until you see results.

---

**"We already rank #1 organically. Why do we need to pay?"**

> If you're already #1, you don't need the Category Sponsor. What you might want is the Featured Sponsor (homepage sidebar) or Premium (newsletter). Those are reach plays, not ranking plays — you're paying to extend your visibility to developers who never made it to your category page.

---

**"What's your traffic?"**

> [Use real numbers when available. Until then:] We don't publish traffic publicly, but I'm happy to share category-level numbers on a call. The honest answer is we're early — but the developers using AgentRank are builders evaluating tools, not casual browsers. We'd rather you see real data and make an informed decision than oversell and lose you after one month.

---

**"$199/mo seems expensive for a new directory."**

> I hear that. Here's the math: if one developer adopts your tool this month because they found you at the top of your category, and that developer is building a product that depends on your tool — what's that worth? For commercial tools with a SaaS tier, the LTV on a single acquired user usually covers the monthly cost. For marketing teams trying to justify spend, it's the price of one sponsored tweet that disappears in 24 hours.

| GitHub Sponsors | Variable | Not a discovery channel |
| Dev.to / Hashnode sponsored posts | $500-2,000/send | One-time reach, not persistent |
| TLDR Newsletter | $2,000-5,000/send | Large audience, not MCP-specific |

**AgentRank's position:**

No other directory charges for MCP-specific placement. We're the only index that scores tools, updates daily, and offers category-level sponsorship with attribution data. The absence of competition makes the sales conversation easier — but it also means we're educating the market, not harvesting existing demand. Early sponsors pay a lower rate and get locked-in pricing before we increase rates when traffic grows.

**The comparable spend:** Founders who understand developer marketing spend $500-2,000/mo on sponsored content. $199/mo for persistent, category-owning placement is underpriced for what it delivers. That's intentional — the goal is to get the first 10 sponsors and build social proof.

## 7. Pricing Justification — Why $199-499/mo Is Right

**The floor ($199/mo):**

$199/mo is low enough that a developer advocate or PM can approve it without a procurement process at most companies. It's in the "no approval needed" range for anyone with a corporate card. This matters for early sales velocity — we need 10 sponsors before we need a formal sales process.

**The ceiling ($499/mo):**

$499/mo is still cheap compared to every alternative (Product Hunt, newsletter sponsorships, conference sponsorships). It's positioned as "less than one sponsored tweet that disappears in 24 hours, for persistent placement that compounds." The ceiling also creates a clear upgrade path: $199 → $299 → $499 as sponsors see ROI.

**Annual pricing (2 months free):**

Annual invoiced contracts are cleaner for the business and create stickiness. Offering 2 months free on annual aligns with standard SaaS annual discount norms (~17% off). This also means we can offer "Net 30 invoicing" — which is often required by enterprise procurement — without needing to integrate a billing system immediately.

**Why not lower (e.g., $49/mo):**

At $49/mo, the tier attracts individual maintainers who have no budget, ask for more support than they're worth, and churn when they don't see immediate ROI. The $199 floor self-selects for companies with a genuine marketing budget and a product to promote. Lower prices also signal lower quality — we want sponsors to feel like they're buying a premium placement, not a banner ad.

**Why not higher (e.g., $999/mo):**

At $999/mo, we'd need to offer SLAs, dedicated account management, and formal reporting. We're not staffed for that yet. More importantly, high prices stall early sales. We need reference customers first. Once 3-5 companies are sponsoring and can vouch for ROI, prices go up.

**Revenue math:**

- 5 Category Sponsors ($199) = $995/mo
- 3 Featured Sponsors ($299) = $897/mo
- 2 Premium Sponsors ($499) = $998/mo
- **10 sponsors = ~$2,890/mo ($34,680 ARR)**

This is meaningful solo-founder revenue. The goal for Q2 is 5 paying sponsors. Q3 goal is 10. Prices increase at the Q3 gate.
